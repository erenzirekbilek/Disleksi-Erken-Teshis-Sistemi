# User Personas - Dyslexia Early Detection System

---

## Persona 1: Teacher

### Demographics
- **Name:** Sarah Mitchell
- **Age:** 34
- **Role:** 3rd Grade Teacher
- **Experience:** 8 years teaching elementary school
- **Tech Proficiency:** Moderate (comfortable with educational software)

### Background
Sarah teaches a class of 28 students. She's noticed that some students struggle with reading and writing but doesn't have the specialized training to diagnose dyslexia. She needs a tool to help identify students who may need further evaluation.

### Goals
- Quickly screen all students for dyslexia risk
- Get actionable insights without extensive training
- Track student progress over time
- Communicate findings effectively to parents
- Integrate seamlessly into existing classroom workflow

### Pain Points
- Limited time for individual assessment
- No access to specialized diagnostic tools
- Difficult to explain concerns to parents without formal diagnosis
- Need to balance screening with teaching responsibilities

### Preferred Features
- Bulk upload for entire class
- Simple dashboard with risk indicators
- Printable reports for parent conferences
- Recommendations for classroom accommodations

### Quote
"I need something quick that doesn't add to my already packed schedule, but gives me reliable information to help my students."

---

## Persona 2: Parent

### Demographics
- **Name:** Maria Rodriguez
- **Age:** 41
- **Role:** Working mother of two
- **Education:** College graduate
- **Tech Proficiency:** High (comfortable with apps and online platforms)

### Background
Maria's 7-year-old son, Lucas, has been struggling with reading and writing. His teacher mentioned he might have dyslexia, but Maria wants more information before pursuing expensive professional assessments. She's looking for early indicators to understand if she should seek further evaluation.

### Goals
- Understand if her child's struggles might indicate dyslexia
- Get clear, non-technical explanations
- Track progress over time
- Share results with healthcare providers
- Find resources for supporting her child at home

### Pain Points
- Conflicting information about dyslexia
- Expensive professional assessments
- Doesn't want to "label" her child unnecessarily
- Needs reassurance and clear next steps
- Time constraints with work and family

### Preferred Features
- Easy-to-understand results
- Plain-language explanations
- Comparison with typical development
- Actionable next steps
- Resources for home support

### Quote
"I just want to know if I should be concerned and what I can do to help my son succeed."

## Persona 3: School Psychologist

### Demographics
- **Name:** Dr. James Chen
- **Age:** 45
- **Role:** School Psychologist
- **Experience:** 15 years in educational psychology
- **Tech Proficiency:** High (uses statistical software and assessment tools)

### Background
Dr. Chen serves three elementary schools with a total of 1,200 students. He conducts formal assessments for students referred by teachers, but the demand far exceeds his capacity. He's looking for a tool to help prioritize referrals and provide preliminary data before formal assessment.

### Goals
- Prioritize students for formal assessment
- Gather preliminary data to inform assessment process
- Document concerns objectively
- Track intervention progress
- Collaborate effectively with teachers and parents

### Pain Points
- Overwhelmed with referral requests
- Time-consuming formal assessments
- Need for objective, quantifiable data
- Difficulty showing progress to stakeholders
- Limited follow-up capabilities

### Preferred Features
- Detailed metrics per modality
- Statistical summaries and trends
- Integration with existing assessment workflows
- Evidence-based reporting
- Longitudinal tracking

### Quote
"I need a tool that provides data-backed insights I can use in my formal assessments and helps me justify my recommendations to parents and administrators."

## User Journey: Teacher (Primary Flow)

```
1. Login → Dashboard
2. Select "Add Students" → Bulk upload CSV
3. Assign reading passages and writing tasks
4. Collect samples (classroom activity)
5. Upload samples → System processes
6. View results → Risk indicators
7. Filter by risk level → Identify concerns
8. Click student → Detailed view
9. Generate report → Print for parent
10. Add notes → Track observations
```

---

## User Journey: Parent (Secondary Flow)

```
1. Receive access code from teacher
2. Login → View child's results
3. See risk indicator → Overall summary
4. Click "View Details" → Modality breakdown
5. Read explanations → Plain language
6. View recommendations → Next steps
7. Download report → Share with doctor
8. Access resources → Home support tips
```

---

## User Journey: Psychologist (Tertiary Flow)

```
1. Login → Dashboard with all students
2. Filter by school → Select grade
3. View class overview → Risk distribution
4. Select student → Full analysis
5. Compare modalities → Side-by-side
6. View temporal trends → Progress
7. Export data → For formal assessment
8. Generate evidence report → PDF
9. Schedule follow-up → Track interventions
```
